# GoldenEye Academy Website Brand Manual

## Positioning

GoldenEye Academy is the practical guidance partner for students and professionals in Pokhara who need a clear next step: course selection, test preparation, digital skills, college guidance, or career readiness.

## Primary Promise

Ask for quick course help first. Choose the right course second.

## Hook Strategy

Every major page, catalogue entry, and campaign should open with a clear hook: a short line that names the visitor's tension and offers a specific next step. The hook must make the visitor feel, "This is for my situation."

Good hooks:

- "Confused after SEE or Plus Two? Do not choose a course randomly."
- "Planning abroad? Pick the right test before you pay for classes."
- "Need a job-ready skill? Start with the course that matches your current level."
- "Parents: get clarity on timing, fees, and outcomes before enrollment."

Hook formula:

1. Name the situation.
2. Show the risk of guessing.
3. Offer quick course help or a practical roadmap.

## Voice

- Clear, confident, and helpful.
- Sales copy should reduce uncertainty, not create pressure.
- Prefer "quick help", "message us", "guidance", "roadmap", "right course", and "next step".
- Avoid exaggerated scarcity unless it is backed by real data.

## CTA Hierarchy

- Primary CTA: "Ask for Course Help"
- Form submit CTA: "Ask for Course Help" or "Get My Guidance"
- WhatsApp CTA: "Message us now"
- Secondary CTA: "View Career Roadmap", "Explore Courses", or "Ask a Question"

Primary CTAs should lead to `route('join-now')`. Secondary informational CTAs can lead to course details, contact, FAQ, or anchors.

## WhatsApp Pattern

Use WhatsApp as a conversation starter, not just an icon. Include:

- Visible label: "Message us now"
- Support text: "Quick reply on WhatsApp"
- Prefilled message: "Hi GoldenEye Academy, I want quick help choosing a course."

## Visual System

- Brand dark: `#050C1C`
- Brand gold: `#C5A059`
- Brand accent: `#A6803B`
- Body text: zinc/neutral grays for long copy
- Primary buttons: gold background with dark text
- Secondary buttons: dark outline or light outline based on section background
- Cards and framed UI: 8px radius
- Pills and badges: fully rounded only when used as compact labels

## Typography

- Headings: Outfit, bold, compact, confident.
- Body: Inter, readable, calm.
- Avoid negative letter spacing in compact UI. Use uppercase sparingly for labels, nav, and badges.

## UX Rules

- Every major page should show one obvious quick-help path above the fold.
- Course cards should offer "View Career Roadmap" before "Ask About This Course".
- Forms should ask for only essential details first.
- Floating CTAs must not overlap the mobile sticky CTA.
- Do not use random seat counts, unsupported certifications, or fake urgency. Use neutral lines such as "Ask early: batches fill quickly" unless real inventory is available.

## Content Defaults

- Hero: "Don't just study. Build your competitive edge."
- Hero body: Explain the outcome and invite a quick message or course-help request.
- Final CTA: "Tell us where you are now. We will help map what comes next."
- Empty CMS states should not use fake programme cards. Seeders must provide real checkpoint content before deployment.
